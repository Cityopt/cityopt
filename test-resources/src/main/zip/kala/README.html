CITYOPT KALASATAMA SIMULATION MODEL DESCRIPTION

 

The Kalasatama model

The Kalasatama simulation model is focusing on alternative utilization options for electricity storages. It contains of two scenarios where the user can specify system properties and input variables (see Figure 1). The first Photovoltaic Battery (PVB) scenario is based on using the storage to store excess electricity produced by solar panels. In the second scenario, Trading Battery (TRB), the storage is used for buying and selling energy based on spot prices. The model is specified according to the storage and photovoltaic capacity of the Ympäristötalo (Helsinki) but can however be modified/scaled to match other places such as Kalasatama. In practice this means that the same weather data, geographical coordinates, cloudiness factor, spot prices, CO2 data and temperature data can be used for southern parts of Finland (such as Kalasatama) while electrical consumption data, storage (electricity) capacity and the photovoltaic capacities need to be defined separately.


Both scenarios share the same data of electricity demand, electricity prices, CO2 emission levels, and solar photovoltaic (PV) production. The TRB-scenario takes in furthermore input data for when to buy and when to sell. All of the mentioned data are from several input files that can be changed depending on user preferences. The original price data is based on European spot market prices for electricity in year 2014.

The main difference between the scenarios is the use purpose of the battery capacity. The battery is given an initial capacity that will drop depending on many times the capacity has been emptied and charged fully again (cycles).

The battery is in the PVB-scenario used for storing excess energy produced by the solar PV system. Excess energy is stored and used directly whenever there is excess or shortage between demand and production. Excess energy is sold to the grid whenever the battery is full.

The Kalasatama model provides for the options to compare the two scenarios in terms of economic profit (), solar fraction (utilized solar power to consumption) and CO2 emission from demand. Furthermore the results also show the CO2 emission difference of the energy sold in comparison to that of the network (energy sold times CO2 levels of energy sold  CO2 levels of the network).

NOTE: The simulation time step is 3600 seconds (one hour) which means that input data are to be adjusted accordingly.
