The Östersundom model

The Östersundom model represents a larger district containing of 14 areas, each with different square meters amounts of multi-storey buildings, town houses, smaller residential buildings, public buildings (schools, kindergarten etc.), commercial buildings and shops. When it comes to energy solution, the whole Östersundom area contains of one combined heat and power (CHP) plant. This plant is controlled according to the heat demand while producing electricity at the same time. There is also a centralized storage for the whole district while each area can also have its own. The size of these storages is modified through their volumes.

 Each area of the district contains, besides of the buildings, of solar collectors, connection pipe to the district heating network, and a heat storage tank. Here it is considered that the area stores excess heat from the solar collectors to the tank for later use. The district pipes contribute to heat losses depending on their length, thickness and temperature difference between outdoor air temperature and temperature of the water flowing in the pipes. The length of each pipe is regarded to be the distance from the CHP to the certain district.

NOTE. There might a limit for some of the user-given values, exceeding these might cause problems with summations since the Östersundom model contains components with thermodynamic properties that fails once unstable.
